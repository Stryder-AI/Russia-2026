Chapter One

The Journey of White Nights & Golden Domes

MOSCOW ◆ ST. PETERSBURG

This is a passage through the two souls of Russia — the red-gold heart of Moscow, and the imperial jewel-box of St. Petersburg — connected by the silver blur of a bullet train skimming across the taiga at 250 km/h.

In Moscow, you walk the ancient cobbles of Red Square beneath the candy-spun domes of St. Basil's. You step into Soviet subway halls that look more like ballrooms, cross the glass sky-bridge of Zaryadye Park, and glide over the city on the VDNKH cable car. In St. Petersburg — the city Peter the Great carved out of a marsh to rival Paris — the palaces do not whisper. They sing.

And all of it unfolds in the long golden twilight of Russia's famous White Nights, when the sun barely sets and the city simply refuses to sleep.

This is not a sightseeing trip. It is a rite of passage — written in gold leaf, cast in bronze, and lit by midsummer skies that never quite go dark.

Day One · Sunday, 28 June 2026

Arrival in Moscow

“Welcome to the Capital of Empires”

Your guide greets you at Sheremetyevo and whisks you straight into the capital. You drift onto the lantern-lit Arbat Street, then slip under the red-and-gold big top of Nikulin's Circus — the legendary 1880 arena. A welcome halal Indian dinner closes your first night on Russian soil.

  • AFTArrive Sheremetyevo Airport (SVO) · private meet & greet · transfer to hotel
  • RESTCheck in · rest and freshen up
  • EVEEvening stroll down the legendary Arbat Street
  • 8 PMNikulin's Circus — premium seating in the green zone
  • LATEWelcome halal Indian dinner · overnight at AZIMUT Aerostar 4★
Arbat Street Nikulin's Circus Welcome Dinner
02
Day Two · Monday, 29 June 2026

Behind the Kremlin Walls

“Where Red Stars and Gold Domes Share the Sky”

Today Russia's beating heart opens to you. Step inside the Kremlin, walk onto the legendary Red Square, and climb into the psychedelic interior of St. Basil's Cathedral. Then descend into the Moscow Metro — stations that look more like ballrooms, built by Stalin as “palaces for the people.”

  • 9 AMBreakfast · meeting with the guide
  • 10 AMInside the Kremlin territory (optional Armoury Chamber $25 p.p.)
  • 12 PMRed Square · St. Basil's Cathedral (interior) · Lenin's Mausoleum · Alexander Garden · GUM · Zaryadye Park with its glass sky-bridge over the Moskva
  • 3 PMMetro Tour — Komsomolskaya, Mayakovskaya, Novoslobodskaya & Kievskaya
  • 8 PMTraditional Russian halal dinner · overnight in Moscow
The Kremlin Red Square St. Basil's Zaryadye Park Metro Palaces
03
Day Three · Tuesday, 30 June 2026

The City of Skylines

“Soviet Monuments & Modern Moscow From Above”

Moscow on a grand scale. Ride up to Sparrow Hills for the postcard view, circle the Gothic rocket of Moscow State University, and pass the golden dome of Christ the Saviour. Afternoon is pure Soviet spectacle at VDNKH — soaring pavilions, gilt statues, and a cable-car ride that floats you above it all.

  • 9 AMBreakfast & meeting with the guide
  • 10 AMPanoramic city tour — Sparrow Hills · MSU · the Seven Sisters (drive-by) · Cathedral of Christ the Saviour · Bolshoi Theatre exterior · Victory Park
  • 2 PMVDNKH Park — monumental pavilions & fountains
  • 4 PMCable car aerial ride over VDNKH
  • 8 PMMediterranean halal dinner · overnight in Moscow
Sparrow Hills MSU Seven Sisters VDNKH Cable Car
04
Day Four · Wednesday, 01 July 2026

The 250 km/h Dash to Imperial Russia

“From Red Square to the Neva”

Leave Moscow aboard the Sapsan silver arrow — 250 km/h, 4 hours, forests and villages slipping past the window. Step onto the platform in St. Petersburg, the Venice of the North, and the air changes. A golden-hour walk down Nevsky Prospekt, then a signature welcome dinner: Imperial Russian cuisine in a heritage venue, White Nights lingering overhead.

  • MORNBreakfast · transfer to Leningradsky Station
  • TRAINSapsan High-Speed Train · 4 hours · up to 250 km/h (comfort class seating)
  • PMArrive St. Petersburg · hotel check-in · rest
  • EVESunset stroll along Nevsky Prospekt — magical under the long White Nights twilight
  • LATESignature welcome halal dinner · Imperial Russian cuisine at a heritage venue (Tamozhnya / Troika)
Sapsan Bullet Train Nevsky Prospekt White Nights Signature Dinner
05
Day Five · Thursday, 02 July 2026

The City of Tsars

“Where Every Facade Tells a Romanov Story”

Today St. Petersburg reveals itself. Cross Palace Square beneath the Winter Palace, pause before the mosaic onion domes of the Church of the Savior on Spilled Blood, meet the Bronze Horseman, and stand under the gold dome of St. Isaac's Cathedral. Close the day on a canal cruise — the way the city was designed to be seen: from the water.

  • 9 AMBreakfast · meeting with the guide
  • 10 AMCity tour — Nevsky Prospekt · the Admiralty · Palace Square & Alexander Column · Church of the Savior on Spilled Blood · St. Isaac's Cathedral · the Bronze Horseman · Rostral Columns · Summer Garden
  • PMNeva River & Canal cruise — the city as Peter the Great meant you to see it
  • 8 PMArabian / Middle Eastern halal dinner · overnight in St. Petersburg
Palace Square Savior on Spilled Blood St. Isaac's Bronze Horseman Canal Cruise
06
Day Six · Friday, 03 July 2026

Fountains & The Amber Room

“The Greatest Palaces in Europe — Twice Over”

Quite possibly the most jaw-dropping day of the journey. Morning: Peterhof — Peter the Great's answer to Versailles, with 64 gravity-fed fountains and 200+ gold-leafed statues. Afternoon: Catherine Palace at Tsarskoye Selo and its legendary Amber Room — six tons of glowing amber, once called the Eighth Wonder. An optional Swan Lake ballet closes the evening.

  • 9 AMBreakfast · meeting with the guide
  • 10 AMPeterhof Park — the Grand Cascade & Upper/Lower gardens (optional hydrofoil one-way $30 p.p.)
  • 2 PMCatherine Palace & the Amber Room at Tsarskoye Selo
  • 8 PMIranian / Persian halal dinner
  • LATEOptional: Swan Lake ballet at Mikhailovsky Theatre (from $150 p.p., transport included)
Peterhof Grand Cascade Catherine Palace Amber Room Swan Lake
07
Day Seven · Saturday, 04 July 2026

A Gentle Goodbye to the Neva

“Coffee, Canals, and a Long Last Look”

After six days of cathedrals and palaces, today belongs to you. Sleep in, nurse a slow coffee, wander Nevsky Prospekt at your own pace — the glittering Eliseev Emporium, Matryoshka dolls, a seat by the Griboedov Canal. Ballet lovers get a second chance at Swan Lake, and the day closes with one last summer twilight along the Neva.

  • MORNBreakfast in the hotel · leisure morning
  • DAYFree time for shopping & strolling — Nevsky Prospekt, Eliseev, souvenir markets
  • 8 PMHalal dinner with soft drinks
  • LATEOptional: Swan Lake ballet at Mikhailovsky Theatre (from $150 p.p.)
Leisure Shopping Nevsky Prospekt Optional Ballet
08
Day Eight · Sunday, 05 July 2026

Farewell to Russia

“With Stories You'll Tell for Decades”

One final Russian breakfast. A last look at the Neva. Your guide accompanies you to Pulkovo Airport (LED) for your onward flight. You leave with a camera roll like a coffee-table book, a head full of tsars and ballerinas, and a group of fellow travellers who now feel like family.

“Do svidaniya, Russia. Thank you for the palaces, the fountains, the chandeliered subways, the White Nights, and most of all for the company that made them matter.”

  • MORNFinal breakfast at the hotel · farewells & photos
  • PMGroup airport transfer · check-in at Pulkovo Airport (LED)
  • EVEEvening flight to Istanbul (IST) — the journey home with an overflowing heart
Pulkovo Airport Onward to IST Farewell
